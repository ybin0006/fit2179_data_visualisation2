Global Overview

Objects Launched Into Space Over Time

For nearly six decades, global space launches maintained a remarkably steady, low-volume baseline. However, the period following 2015 marks the dawn of the "New Space" era, characterized by an unprecedented exponential explosion in objects sent into orbit.

Primary Mission Objectives

An overwhelming imbalance in the global satellite ecosystem can be seen from this chart. The vast majority of satellites orbiting Earth are dedicated to a single purpose: Communications. Traditional space missions like scientific discovery and global navigation are heavily eclipsed by our massive global demand for commercial connectivity and planet imaging.

Operator Country vs Contractor Country

Most major spacefaring nations act as their own primary contractors. For superpowers like the USA and China.
